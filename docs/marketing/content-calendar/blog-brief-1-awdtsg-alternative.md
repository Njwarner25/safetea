**Q1: Is AWDTSG still active in 2026?**
> Yes, in a fragmented way. Many city-specific Facebook groups have re-formed under new names after Meta deletions. The original network is no longer centralized, and finding the active group for your city is harder than it used to be. Many former AWDTSG members now use a combination of a dedicated safety platform and a city subreddit.

**Q2: What is the closest direct replacement for AWDTSG?**
> SafeTea was designed specifically as a successor to the AWDTSG model: city-based safety communities, structured posts about people you have matched with, anonymous by default, and built outside of Meta's moderation system. It is the closest one-to-one functional replacement available in 2026.

**Q3: Is the Tea app the same thing as AWDTSG?**
> Not exactly. The Tea app shares the goal of helping women vet potential dates, but its structure is different — it allows real names and operates more like a public review system. AWDTSG was built on closed Facebook groups with private discussion. SafeTea is closer to the original AWDTSG model in privacy posture.

**Q4: Can I use more than one of these platforms?**
> Yes, and many people do. A dedicated safety platform handles structured reports about specific profiles, while a city subreddit is useful for general dating-safety questions. Each tool has a different best use.

**Q5: Why did Facebook keep deleting AWDTSG groups?**
> Mass reporting campaigns, often coordinated, triggered Facebook's automated removal systems. Once a group was flagged enough times, it was deleted regardless of whether the reports had merit. There was no meaningful appeals process. This is one of the core reasons the movement has shifted to platforms that do not depend on Meta.
