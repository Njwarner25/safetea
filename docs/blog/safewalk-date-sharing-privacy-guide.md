# How SafeWalk Helps You Share Your Date Without Sharing Your Privacy

Everyone knows the drill. Before a first date, you text your best friend the name of the restaurant, a screenshot of the person's profile, and something like "if I don't text you by 10, call me." It works, sort of. But it relies on your friend actually checking their phone, it gives them no real-time information if something goes wrong, and it does nothing if you forget to send the text in the first place.

SafeWalk was designed to replace that improvised safety system with something purpose-built, reliable, and private.

## What SafeWalk Actually Does

SafeWalk is a real-time date safety feature that lets you share your date details with up to five trusted contacts. When you activate SafeWalk before a date, your chosen contacts receive a secure notification with the information you choose to share. Throughout the date, the system monitors for check-ins and provides your trusted circle with updates without requiring you to do anything beyond a quick tap.

Here is what makes it different from texting a friend or sharing your location on Google Maps.

## Privacy by Design

The biggest concern people have about location-sharing tools is that they feel invasive. Sharing your live location on Find My Friends or Google Maps gives someone continuous access to your movements, often long after the date ends. Social media check-ins broadcast your location to everyone, not just the people you trust.

SafeWalk takes a different approach. Your date details are shared only with contacts you explicitly select. There is no social media integration, no broadcasting, and no persistent tracking. When the date ends and you close out the SafeWalk session, sharing stops immediately. Your trusted contacts cannot see your location outside of active SafeWalk sessions.

The data itself is encrypted and is not stored after the session ends. SafeTea never sells location data or uses it for advertising. Your date is your business, and SafeWalk is designed to keep it that way.

## Timed Check-Ins

Before starting a SafeWalk session, you set a check-in interval. This might be every 30 minutes, every hour, or a custom timeframe. At each interval, SafeWalk sends you a discreet notification asking you to confirm you are okay. A single tap confirms everything is fine.

If you miss a check-in, your trusted contacts are automatically notified. They receive your last known location and the date details you shared at the start of the session. This passive system means safety happens in the background. You do not need to remember to text anyone, and your contacts do not need to sit by their phone waiting.

## The Panic Button

Every SafeWalk session includes access to a panic button. If you feel unsafe at any point during a date, pressing the panic button triggers an immediate alert to all your trusted contacts with your current location. Depending on your settings, it can also initiate a call to emergency services.

The panic button is designed to be accessible quickly but not so exposed that it could be triggered accidentally. It sits behind a single deliberate gesture, ensuring it is there when you need it without creating false alarms.

## How to Use SafeWalk: Step by Step

**Step 1: Set Up Trusted Contacts.** In the SafeTea app, go to Settings and select Trusted Contacts. Add up to five people by phone number or email. Each contact receives an invitation to download the SafeTea companion notification system. They do not need a full SafeTea account.

**Step 2: Create a SafeWalk Session.** Before your date, tap Start SafeWalk on the home screen. Enter the details you want to share: the venue name, address, your date's first name, and optionally a screenshot of their profile.

**Step 3: Set Your Check-In Schedule.** Choose how often you want SafeWalk to check in with you. For a coffee date, every 30 minutes might work. For a dinner date, every hour is typical. You can always adjust mid-session.

**Step 4: Go on Your Date.** SafeWalk runs quietly in the background. When a check-in notification appears, tap to confirm you are fine. That is it.

**Step 5: End the Session.** When your date is over, open SafeTea and tap End SafeWalk. Your trusted contacts receive a notification that you have ended the session, and all location sharing stops immediately.

## Why It Is Better Than the Alternatives

Texting a friend works until it does not. Your friend might be asleep, their phone might be on silent, or you might forget to send the initial text. Location sharing through Apple or Google broadcasts your movements 24/7, which most people find uncomfortable.

SafeWalk is built specifically for dating. It activates only when you need it, shares only what you choose, and has automated failsafes that do not depend on someone actively monitoring their phone. It is the difference between an improvised solution and a purpose-built safety system.
