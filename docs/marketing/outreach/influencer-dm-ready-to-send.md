# SafeTea — Ready-to-Send Influencer DMs

**Purpose:** Copy-paste DMs you can send today. Each is paired with the creator persona it's designed for, the platform it's written for, and the personalization slot you'll need to fill.

**The strategy:** Gift first, ask never (in the first message). The first DM offers a free SafeTea+ subscription with no strings. Anything else burns the contact. Paid collab discussion happens *only* in the warm follow-up after they've used the product.

**Disclosure:** The first DM doesn't need an FTC disclosure (no compensation yet). Once they accept any compensation — flat fee, free product retained beyond a trial, affiliate link — every public post must carry `#ad` or `#sponsored` per FTC guidelines. This is non-negotiable.

**Outreach cadence:** Send 5 cold DMs/day, 5 days/week = 25/week. Track opens and replies in a simple spreadsheet (creator handle, sent date, replied y/n, sent gift y/n, paid collab y/n). Don't follow up on a non-reply more than once.

**Block list:** Do NOT cold-DM creators who have publicly criticized AWDTSG or women's-safety platforms in the last 90 days. They'll screenshot you. Always check the creator's recent content before sending.

## What NOT to send

These are the templates we've explicitly chosen to NOT include because they backfire:

- **The "exclusive partnership" pitch.** Smells like a 2015 affiliate-marketer cold email. Don't.
- **The "I'd love to chat" with no specifics.** Wastes everyone's time. Always have a concrete offer in the first message.
- **The voice-message DM on Instagram.** Feels intrusive. Text only.
- **The "we have similar audiences!" line.** Cringe. Show, don't claim.
- **Anything with the words "synergy," "leverage," "amplify," or "unlock."** Marketing-speak triggers the spam reflex.
