# SafeTea x Women's Safety Nonprofits — Partnerships Brief

*Owner: Marketing / Partnerships. Status: outreach brief, ready for cohort-1 cold outreach.*

---

## 1. Why this channel

Women's safety nonprofits already hold the trust SafeTea is trying to earn. A modest mention in the right newsletter or a co-authored guide on the right blog reaches an audience that is pre-qualified, attentive, and primed for our voice. The channel is small in raw volume but unusually high in intent: someone reading a Stop Street Harassment newsletter is already thinking about safety, and a soft handoff to SafeTea is a much warmer introduction than any paid impression.

Equally important: these are sensitive partners. Several of them work with survivors. Several have spent decades being pitched by tech companies looking for a halo. Our default is humility, restraint, and a long timeline — not a sales motion.

## 3. Partnership Offer Template

What we put on the table for partner orgs. The default offer is the same across Tier 1; Tier 2 gets the resource-listing version only.

**Standard offer (Tier 1):**

- **Free SafeTea+ for their members** — unique partner code that grants 6 months of SafeTea+ Pro to anyone who redeems via the partner's channels. No cap on redemptions for the first 12 months. No data shared back to the partner beyond an aggregate redemption count.
- **Co-marketed content** — one of: a newsletter swap, a co-authored guide hosted on both sites, or a joint webinar/Instagram Live. Partner approves all copy and visuals before publish.
- **Editorial control** — the partner has final-cut on anything that mentions their name, their logo, or quotes their staff. SafeTea will not publish without explicit sign-off.
- **No data exchange** — we never ask partners to share their member list. We never share SafeTea user data with them.
- **No exclusivity** — they're free to partner with anyone else, including competitors.
- **Quiet exit** — if at any point the partnership stops feeling right to them, we end it within 30 days, no penalty, no pressure to explain.

**Resource-listing offer (Tier 2):**

- Same SafeTea+ Pro access for staff (not for their member-facing channels).
- Request to be listed on their dating-safety resource page, only if they think we belong there.
- No co-marketing, no joint content, no public announcement of the relationship.

## 5. Three Co-Marketing Campaigns

### Campaign A — Newsletter swap (lowest lift, fastest)

- **Format:** Each side writes a 150-word blurb introducing the other to their newsletter audience. Both run in the same week.
- **SafeTea blurb places in partner newsletter:** introduces SafeTea as a free dating-safety community, includes the partner-coded link to claim 6 months of SafeTea+ Pro.
- **Partner blurb places in SafeTea newsletter:** introduces their org's work, includes their preferred CTA (donate, volunteer, sign up for their list).
- **Approvals:** both sides see and sign off on the other's blurb before send. Hard rule.
- **Best fit:** Stop Street Harassment, Hollaback!, Cybersmile.

### Campaign B — Joint webinar (medium lift, highest engagement)

- **Format:** 45-minute live conversation, recorded and republished. Topic example: *"Dating-app safety in 2026: what's actually changed, and what hasn't."*
- **Speakers:** SafeTea founder + the partner's lead expert in the relevant area.
- **Promotion:** both sides promote in the two weeks prior. Recording goes on both YouTube channels and gets transcribed into a co-authored blog post.
- **CTA:** soft. SafeTea is mentioned by name once at the start and once at the end. No mid-roll pitch.
- **Best fit:** Cyber Civil Rights Initiative, Cybersmile, Right To Be.

### Campaign C — Co-authored guide (highest lift, longest tail)

- **Format:** A 2,500–3,500-word resource hosted on both sites. Working title: *"The Pre-Date Vetting Guide: What Women Actually Do, and How to Do It Without Doxxing Anyone."*
- **Authorship:** drafted by SafeTea's content lead, edited by the partner's subject-matter expert, both bylines.
- **Distribution:** each side publishes on the same day, with canonical SEO tags pointing to whichever side has stronger domain authority for the keyword. Newsletter feature in both. Cross-linked from each org's resource library.
- **Tone bar:** smart, protective, calm. No "horror story" framing. No statistics without sources. Anything sourced from a survivor's story requires explicit consent.
- **Best fit:** Cybersmile, Cyber Civil Rights Initiative, NNEDV (only if they invite the depth).

## 6. Compliance and Sensitivity Notes

These guardrails apply to every partnership conversation, every piece of co-marketed content, and every internal decision about which orgs to approach.

- **No aggressive sales tactics.** No follow-up cadences, no "circling back" emails, no calendaring tools that trick someone into booking. One outreach email; one polite second touch after 14 days if there's no reply; then we stop. Permanently.
- **Survivor-serving orgs are not lead sources.** RAINN affiliates, NNEDV, NSVRC, and DV coalitions exist to support survivors. We do not pitch their members. We do not ask them to drive signups. We ask only to be considered as a listed resource.
- **No survivor stories in marketing.** SafeTea will not solicit, repurpose, or co-publish first-person survivor stories for marketing — even with consent. Stories belong to the people who lived them. Our content is structural ("how vetting works," "what to look for") not narrative.
- **No scare-statistic openers.** Co-authored content and webinars do not lead with sexual-violence prevalence statistics. We respect the audience's intelligence and don't trade in fear.
- **No SafeTea logo on partner crisis materials.** If a partner runs a hotline page, an emergency-resources page, or anything survivors might encounter in crisis, our logo and CTAs do not belong there. SafeTea is a community-vetting tool, not a crisis resource. Confusing the two could harm someone in a real moment.
- **No exchange of user data.** Ever. We don't ask partners for their list. We don't share ours.
- **Editorial veto belongs to the partner.** If they want a line removed, it's removed. If they want the partnership unpublished, we unpublish within 48 hours.
- **Privacy-first review.** Before any partner publishes a SafeTea-branded asset, our privacy lead reviews to ensure nothing in the creative undermines the no-real-names, no-real-photos brand promise.
- **Affiliate disclosure.** If any partnership involves compensation in either direction (including in-kind value above $500), both sides disclose per FTC endorsement guidelines.
- **Crisis routing.** Anyone reaching out to SafeTea in crisis through a partner channel is routed to RAINN (1-800-656-HOPE) and to local resources. SafeTea responders are trained to do this in the first reply, not the third.
