# SafeTea Campus Ambassador Program

*Owner: Marketing / Partnerships. Status: program brief, ready to recruit cohort 1 (10 universities, 1–2 ambassadors each).*

---

## 1. Program Overview

The SafeTea Campus Ambassadors are a small, paid cohort of student leaders — primarily upperclass women — who help bring SafeTea's dating safety community to their campus. Ambassadors are not salespeople. They're trusted local voices who host one or two thoughtful events per month, distribute safety-forward materials (QR posters, dorm flyers, women's-center handouts), and seed SafeTea inside the networks they already belong to: Greek life, club sports, women in STEM groups, RA networks, and survivor-support orgs. We pick urban, dating-app-saturated campuses with large female undergraduate populations because that's where the AWDTSG audience already exists and where word-of-mouth compounds fastest.

---

## 2. Ambassador Responsibilities

- Host **one campus activation per month** (tabling at the student union, a women's-center co-event, a Greek-life chapter visit, or a dorm-floor tea night). SafeTea provides the kit; ambassadors run the room.
- Distribute SafeTea materials across **5 high-traffic campus locations** per term — women's center, student health, library bulletin boards, Greek-row houses, and the rec center. Posters and QR flyers are pre-designed.
- **Publish 2 organic social posts per month** (Instagram Story or TikTok) about a real dating-safety topic, tagging @safe_teaapp on Instagram and @safetea_official on TikTok. No scripted ad copy; ambassadors keep their voice.
- Recruit and onboard **at least one campus partner org per semester** — sorority chapter, women's-center programming office, RA training coordinator, or a survivor advocacy group.
- Submit a short **monthly check-in** (Loom or Google Form): what worked, what didn't, signup count, and any safety concerns surfaced on campus.

## 4. Application Form (10 Questions)

These are written to filter for trust, judgment, and existing community access — not hustle.

1. Name, university, expected graduation year, and primary major.
2. In 3–4 sentences: why does dating safety matter to you personally or to your community?
3. Which on-campus groups are you actively part of? (Greek life, club, sports team, advocacy org, residence-life role, etc.)
4. Are you comfortable being a public-facing voice for SafeTea on your campus? (Yes / No / It depends — please explain.)
5. Have you ever heard of "Are We Dating The Same Guy?" Facebook groups? If yes, what's your honest take on them?
6. Tell us about a time you helped a friend think through a dating-safety situation. What did you do?
7. Link to your most-active social account (Instagram or TikTok) — we look at engagement, not follower count.
8. How many hours per week can you realistically commit between now and the end of the term?
9. Are you able to host or co-host one in-person campus event per month? Any constraints we should know about?
10. SafeTea is a privacy-first platform — no real names, no real photos. If a friend asked you to "expose someone" by name, how would you respond?

Q5, Q6, and Q10 are the load-bearing questions. We're screening *out* anyone whose instinct is to escalate or publicly shame, and screening *in* people who naturally de-escalate and protect.

## 6. Onboarding Playbook

### Week 1 — Orientation and brand grounding
- 60-min Zoom kickoff with the partnerships lead. Walk through SafeTea's positioning, the "smart, protective, calm" voice, what we never do (no real names, no fearmongering, no shaming).
- Ship the ambassador kit: 50 QR flyers, 10 large posters, 25 sticker packs, ambassador one-pager, branded tote.
- Ambassador sets up referral code at safetea.app/ambassadors and tests it.
- Read assignment: SafeTea's three published blog posts and the AWDTSG explainer.

### Week 2 — Soft launch in their inner circle
- Ambassador invites 10–20 close friends to join SafeTea using their referral code. Goal is voice calibration, not volume — they learn how to talk about it before they're public.
- 30-min 1:1 with another ambassador (peer pairing). Compare notes, share scripts.
- First Instagram Story or TikTok goes live (template provided, voice is theirs).

### Week 3 — Campus partnerships
- Ambassador reaches out to 1 women's-center contact and 1 Greek-life or club contact using the warm-intro template (provided).
- First poster + QR flyer drop across 5 locations.
- Submit a short photo recap — this is the deliverable that confirms materials are out.

### Week 4 — First activation
- Host first event. Suggested formats: tabling at student union with branded tea (low lift), women's-center co-hosted "safer dating night" (medium lift), Greek chapter visit with 15-min intro + Q&A (highest conversion).
- Submit month-1 check-in: signup count, what worked, what didn't, any campus concerns.
- Schedule month-2 activation. Cadence is now monthly.

## 7. Activation Tactics

### Poster designs (3 variants, all tested by the design team)
1. **"Stay connected, stay safe."** — minimal type-driven poster, large QR, illustrated avatar. The default.
2. **"Before the second date."** — copy block listing 5 things SafeTea helps with (community vetting, red-flag library, anonymous notes). For women's-center bulletin boards.
3. **"For us, by us."** — founder-voice poster with the line "built by women who got tired of warning each other in DMs." For Greek houses and women-in-STEM spaces.

All three carry the URL **safetea.app** and a QR code. No App Store badges. The QR resolves to a campus-tagged landing page (`/c/<campus-slug>`) so we can attribute signups by school.

### QR-code flyers
- Half-sheet quarter-page format, designed to fit in a folder or tape to a dorm door.
- Front: hook + QR. Back: 4-step "what SafeTea actually does" + privacy promise.
- Distribution: 50 per ambassador per month. Refill kits ship monthly.

### Greek-life partnerships
- Target the **VP of Risk Management** or **Sisterhood Chair** in each chapter — these roles already own member safety and welcome external resources.
- Offer a 15-minute chapter visit, branded tea bags for the meeting, and SafeTea+ Pro codes for every member who joins.
- Avoid the "philanthropy partner" framing — SafeTea is a tool, not a cause they sponsor.

### Women's center co-marketing
- Most campus women's centers run "healthy relationships" or "safer dating" programming each semester. Position SafeTea as a free resource they can hand to students, not as a vendor pitching them.
- Offer: a co-branded one-pager the center can distribute, optional 30-min guest segment in their existing programming, and SafeTea+ Pro for staff so they can evaluate it firsthand.
- Sensitive note: never co-market with a survivor-support office without explicit invitation. SafeTea is dating-safety, not crisis response, and we don't want to be confused with one.

## 9. What we don't do

- We don't ask ambassadors to share screenshots, names, or stories from AWDTSG groups in their own posts.
- We don't pay per-event-attendee bounties — that turns ambassadors into recruiters under pressure.
- We don't ship to campuses where SafeTea has zero existing users; ambassadors deserve a product their friends can actually find traction in.
- We don't market SafeTea as crisis support. If an ambassador surfaces a serious situation on campus, we route to RAINN (1-800-656-HOPE) and the campus Title IX office.
