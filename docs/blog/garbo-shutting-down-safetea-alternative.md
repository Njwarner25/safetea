# Garbo Is Shutting Down: What It Means for Dating Safety

In early 2026, Garbo announced it would be winding down operations, marking the end of one of the most ambitious experiments in dating safety technology. For the millions of people who relied on Garbo to screen potential dates, the news raises an urgent question: what comes next?

## What Garbo Did and Why It Mattered

Garbo launched in 2018 as a nonprofit background check platform designed specifically for the dating context. Founded by Kathryn Kosmides, a survivor of gender-based violence, the platform aimed to democratize access to public records that were previously only available through expensive or cumbersome services.

The premise was straightforward. Enter a first name and phone number, and Garbo would search public records for arrests, convictions, sex offender registry entries, and restraining orders. The service gained significant traction when Tinder integrated it directly into its app in 2022, giving users one-tap access to background checks on their matches.

At its peak, Garbo represented something bigger than a single product. It signaled that the dating industry was finally taking user safety seriously, moving beyond profile verification badges and into substantive safety infrastructure.

## Why Garbo Is Closing

While Garbo has not disclosed every detail behind the decision, several factors contributed to the shutdown. Running a nonprofit in a space dominated by well-funded for-profit companies proved financially unsustainable. The costs of maintaining accurate, up-to-date public records databases are enormous, and the subscription model struggled to generate enough revenue to cover operational expenses.

There were also ongoing challenges around data accuracy. Background check databases are notoriously inconsistent across jurisdictions. A clean report from Garbo did not necessarily mean a person had no record; it meant no record was found in the databases Garbo could access. This gap between user expectations and technical reality created liability concerns.

Additionally, the partnership with Tinder, while groundbreaking, did not expand to other major platforms at the scale needed to sustain the business.

## The Gap Garbo Leaves Behind

Garbo's shutdown leaves a real void in the dating safety landscape. Before Garbo, running a background check on a date meant navigating sketchy websites with questionable data, paying premium prices for services designed for employers, or simply going without. Garbo made safety checks accessible and normalized the idea that screening a date is not paranoid but prudent.

Without Garbo, millions of daters lose a tool they trusted. And the need has not diminished. According to a 2025 Pew Research study, roughly 3 in 10 adults in the U.S. have used a dating app, and concerns about safety remain the top reason people hesitate to try online dating.
